WAIRE Program

What Is the WAIRE Program?

The Warehouse Actions and Investments to Reduce Emissions (WAIRE) Program (also known as Rule 2305) is a California Air Resources Board initiative aimed at reducing greenhouse gas emissions and other air pollutants linked to warehouse operations in the state.

To comply with the program, warehouses must implement zero-emission technologies, optimize shipping and receiving operations, and improve energy efficiency.

How It Works

How Does the WAIRE Program Work?

The WAIRE Program is a menu-based point system that promotes environmentally friendly warehouse operations in California. Warehouses must earn a certain number of points based on their total floor area or for investing in eligible emission reduction projects.

Failure to earn the required number of points results in mitigation fees based on the number of deficient points. Our team can help you understand and meet these requirements efficiently.

Compliance

Who Must Comply?

Owners and operators of warehouses in the South Coast AQMD with 100,000 square feet or more of indoor floor space in a single building must comply with Rule 2305. This includes public and private warehouses, distribution centers, and fulfillment centers.

Warehouse operators may be exempt from parts of the rule if they operate less than 50,000 square feet for warehousing activities or if the number of required points is less than 10.
