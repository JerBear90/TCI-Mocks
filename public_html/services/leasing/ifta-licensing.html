What Is IFTA Fuel Tax Reporting?

The International Fuel Tax Agreement (IFTA) requires motor carriers operating in multiple jurisdictions to report fuel purchases and miles traveled in each state or province. Quarterly IFTA returns calculate the net tax owed to or refunded from each jurisdiction based on the difference between fuel purchased and fuel consumed within its borders.

For fleets operating across state lines, IFTA compliance involves meticulous record-keeping of every fuel receipt, odometer reading, and route traveled. Errors or late filings trigger penalties, interest charges, and potential audit exposure. TCI's compliance team manages the entire IFTA process for every vehicle in your lease fleet — from data collection and mileage tracking through quarterly filing and payment reconciliation.

Risk Reduction

Why IFTA & Licensing Management Matters

IFTA non-compliance carries real consequences. Late filings incur penalties of $50 or more per vehicle per quarter, plus interest on unpaid taxes. Repeated violations can result in IFTA license revocation, which effectively grounds your interstate fleet. State auditors can go back four years, and discrepancies between reported and actual mileage trigger assessments that include back taxes, penalties, and audit fees.

Beyond IFTA, commercial vehicles require IRP (International Registration Plan) credentials, UCR (Unified Carrier Registration), HVUT (Heavy Vehicle Use Tax) filings, and state-specific permits. Each has its own deadlines, renewal cycles, and documentation requirements. TCI's licensing team tracks every credential for every vehicle in your lease fleet, ensuring nothing expires, nothing gets missed, and your trucks never get pulled off the road for a paperwork issue.

How TCI Manages Your IFTA & Licensing

TCI integrates fuel card data, GPS mileage tracking, and driver trip reports to build accurate, audit-ready IFTA records for every vehicle in your lease fleet. Our compliance team reconciles fuel purchases against jurisdictional miles quarterly, prepares and files all IFTA returns, and manages tax payments and refund credits on your behalf.

For licensing and registration, TCI maintains a master calendar of every credential expiration date across your fleet. We handle IRP renewals, cab card updates, UCR filings, HVUT Form 2290 submissions, and state-specific permit applications. When you add or remove vehicles from your lease, we update all registrations and credentials automatically. The result is a fleet that's always legal, always compliant, and never at risk of a roadside shutdown due to expired paperwork.
