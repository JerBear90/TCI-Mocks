## What Is Intermodal Freight Brokerage?
Intermodal freight brokerage coordinates the movement of cargo using two or more modes of transportation — typically rail for the long-haul linehaul segment and truck for the first-mile and last-mile drayage. Freight is loaded into intermodal containers or trailers that transfer seamlessly between truck chassis and rail flatcars without the cargo itself being handled.
TCI's intermodal brokerage division manages the entire door-to-door process: arranging drayage pickup at your facility, booking rail capacity with Class I railroad partners, coordinating the container transfer at origin and destination rail terminals, and scheduling final-mile delivery to the consignee. You get a single rate, a single point of contact, and end-to-end visibility — with the cost advantages of rail for the longest segment of the journey.
## Why Intermodal Saves on Long-Haul Freight
Rail is inherently more fuel-efficient than over-the-road trucking — a single train can move one ton of freight over 470 miles on a single gallon of fuel. For shipments traveling 500 miles or more, intermodal typically delivers 10-40% cost savings compared to full truckload rates, with the greatest savings on transcontinental lanes.
Beyond cost, intermodal provides capacity stability that pure trucking cannot match. When driver shortages and tight truck markets drive spot rates skyward, rail capacity remains relatively stable and predictably priced. For companies with sustainability commitments, intermodal reduces greenhouse gas emissions by up to 75% compared to over-the-road trucking on equivalent lanes — a measurable contribution to your environmental goals that you can report to stakeholders.
## How TCI Coordinates Your Intermodal Shipments
TCI begins by analyzing your freight lanes to identify which routes are strong candidates for intermodal conversion. We evaluate origin-destination pairs, transit time requirements, volume consistency, and commodity characteristics to build a lane-by-lane recommendation that maximizes savings without compromising service.
For each intermodal shipment, TCI coordinates drayage carriers at both ends, books rail capacity with our railroad partners, and monitors the container through every handoff point. Our tracking system provides real-time location updates as the container moves from truck to rail to truck, with proactive alerts for any delays or exceptions. If a rail service disruption occurs, TCI has contingency plans ready — including over-the-road backup — to protect your delivery commitments.
