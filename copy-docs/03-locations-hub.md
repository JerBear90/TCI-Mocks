## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is a full-service lease?**
A full-service lease is an off-balance sheet lease that includes maintenance, unscheduled repairs, tires, 24/7 road service and support, and substitute vehicles when the truck is down for mechanical repairs. Additional services such as safety, IFTA fuel tax reporting, electronic trip reporting systems, truck washing, and licensing/registration can also be included in your lease program.

**What are the benefits of a full-service lease over ownership?**
Full-service leasing provides cash flow benefits and saves your working capital for your company's core competencies. It reduces the administrative burdens of operating a fleet and eliminates the costly overhead of a shop and the OSHA and CARB compliance expenses of maintaining that shop. For smaller fleets that do not operate their own shops, it provides a predictable maintenance expense to budget with.

**What type of equipment do you offer?**
TCI offers a variety of vehicles from cargo vans to tractors and trailers to meet your needs. In addition, we offer Ottawa yard spotter tractors and electric vehicles. TCI will tailor the specifications of these vehicles to meet your application.

**What is the difference between a full-service lease and a long-term rental?**
The services being offered are essentially the same. Our rental program is full service but rates are based on a time period of a day to up to a year. With a full-service lease, you can have a truck built to your exact specifications with a lease rate that is discounted based on the length of the term and annual miles.

**Does TCI offer maintenance services?**
Yes, TCI offers contract maintenance services. If you own your own truck fleet but do not have the facilities or personnel to maintain your fleet, TCI can provide you with a comprehensive preventative maintenance program that can include on-site maintenance and 24/7 breakdown support.

**Why has dedicated contract carriage become so popular?**
Outsourcing transportation removes the risk and cost of maintaining and purchasing a fleet, keeping up with regulations and compliance, and recruiting and managing drivers. This allows companies to focus on what they do best, which is managing their core business.

**What are the most common freight management options?**
Private carriage is when shippers elect to manage their own trucks and drivers. Common carriage is freight moved by multiple third-party trucking carriers on an as-needed and transactional basis. Dedicated contract carriage is when shippers outsource the management and operation of their transportation needs to a third-party logistics provider (3PL), who operates and maintains the truck fleet, hires and manages the drivers, and ensures safe, on-time delivery.

**What type of equipment does TCI sell?**
Our used truck division offers a wide variety of trucks including over-the-road tractors, medium duty trucks (bobtails, stake beds, and reefers), vocational trucks (dump trucks, water trucks, and roll-off units), and dry and reefer vans.
