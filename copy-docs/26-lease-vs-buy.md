## Understanding the Lease vs. Buy Decision
When your business needs commercial trucks, the fundamental question is whether to purchase vehicles outright or enter a full-service lease agreement. Purchasing means large upfront capital expenditures, depreciation on your balance sheet, and full responsibility for maintenance, compliance, and eventual resale. A full-service lease from TCI converts those variable, unpredictable costs into a single fixed monthly payment.
The right answer depends on your business model, cash flow position, fleet utilization rates, and how much operational complexity you want to manage internally. TCI's lease vs. buy analysis examines all of these factors to give you a clear, data-driven recommendation.
## Leasing vs. Buying: A Side-by-Side Comparison
Here's how a TCI full-service lease stacks up against purchasing your own fleet.
Full-service leasing provides significant cash flow benefits. It saves your working capital to be used on your company's core competencies. Lease fleets reduce the administrative burdens of operating the fleet and eliminate the costly overhead of a shop and the OSHA and CARB compliance expenses of maintaining that shop.
For smaller fleets that do not operate their own shops, a full-service lease provides a predictable maintenance expense to budget with. No down payment is required on a TCI full-service lease — just a well-established commercial credit history. Lease terms typically run between 36 and 84 months depending on the application and annual miles.
